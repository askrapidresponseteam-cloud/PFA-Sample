About PFA

People for Animals is India's largest non-governmental animal welfare organisation, with a nationwide network of 26 hospitals, 165 units, 60 mobile units and over 2.5 lakh members.

We rescue and rehabilitate sick and needy animals. We set up and run shelters, ambulance services, sterilisation programmes, treatment camps and disaster rescue missions. We run education programmes in schools, fight cases in court, and lobby on animal issues in parliament.

Our aim is to set up an animal welfare centre in each of the nation's 600 districts.

Sanjay Gandhi Animal Care Centre, a 24-hour hospital and shelter that houses over 3000 animals at any given time.

Visit the centre

Inside the centre

01In-Patient Department

A 24-hour facility that houses over 3000 animals at any given point, with dedicated wards for recovery and long-term care.

02Out-Patient Department

A round-the-clock service supported by an animal ambulance that works through the night to reach animals in distress.

03Intensive Care Unit

Separate ICU centres for both large and small animals, equipped to stabilise the most critical cases.

04Operation Theatre

The hub of the centre's ongoing Animal Birth Control programme and the home of all surgical care.

05Laboratory

Equipped with an x-ray machine and blood-testing facilities to support fast, accurate diagnosis.

06Animal Birth Control

One of our key programmes, humanely managing street animal populations through sterilisation and vaccination.

07Animal Cemetery

One of Delhi's only dedicated burial grounds for animals, offering a dignified farewell.

Chairperson's message

Form part of the magic circle of love around all life.

Each species, from bees to elephants, creates a protective shield for humans. The quality of our lives depends on them. When we eat them, exploit them, interfere with natural patterns by breeding one kind and killing another, we destroy our own lives. As each species disappears, our quality of life becomes worse.

You can help. You can help us to help. You can form part of the magic circle of love around all life.

People for Animals works to create a kinder, gentler world for all. Formed in 1994, it has transformed animal welfare into an assertive, effective movement across the country. Our members include lawyers, film stars, business leaders, bureaucrats, teachers and students. Our work ranges from running shelters and ambulances to lobbying, fighting for animals in court, training judges, bureaucrats and police, and producing information material. We have won victories for animals in court, in research laboratories, racecourses, temples, circuses and zoos.

PFA is regarded as one of the strongest animal groups in the world. When the world's first International Animal Welfare Directory was compiled, its UK publishers invited PFA to write the foreword. At home, in 2001 the first Padma Shri award for animal welfare went to a PFA member, Dr. Norma Alvares.

Humans cannot exist without animals, yet we create a world in which they are abused and misused. Let us recognise the partnership between us and further it for the good of all animals, both on two legs and four.

- Smt. Maneka Sanjay Gandhi
